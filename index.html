Exit Strategy

Last updated April 21, 2026

A disciplined, rules-based system to protect capital, secure profits, and let exceptional winners run their full course. This protocol eliminates emotional decisions by providing exact triggers, exact stop placement, and exact actions for every phase of a trade's lifecycle. Applies to mid-to-long term position trades.

Minervini (SEPA) Darvas (Box Theory) O'Neil (CANSLIM) Kullamagi (Qullamaggie)

Core Principles

Expand All Collapse All
1
Hard Max Loss: 6% from entry. ▼
Target average loss: 4%. Aim to place stops around 4% from entry. 6% is the absolute ceiling — if a trade hits -6%, you are wrong, exit immediately.

ATR buffer: Place stops at <1× ATR(14) below your confluence reference to avoid stop hunts. Less buffer is better — use only what's needed to survive normal noise.

How they connect: First, identify your confluence support level. Then subtract the ATR buffer. Finally, verify the total distance from entry does not exceed 6%. If it does, either wait for a tighter entry or pass on the trade entirely.
2
Max dollar risk per trade: $2,000 USD. ▼
The unit of risk is $2K. This is the maximum dollar amount you accept to lose per trade unit (1 portion = ~$40K). A position can be multiple portions — 2 portions ($80K) allows $4K risk, 3 portions ($120K) allows $6K risk.

How it relates to Principle 1: Principle 1 limits how far your stop can be from entry (max 6%). This principle caps the actual dollars at stake. Together they determine your position size — if the stop distance and dollar risk don't align, reduce shares until both rules are satisfied.
3
Portfolio risk cap: ≤ 5% of total portfolio value. ▼
Sum the distance-to-stop across all open positions (in dollar terms). That total must not exceed 5% of your total portfolio value. This prevents a scenario where multiple correlated positions all hit their stops simultaneously and create an outsized portfolio drawdown.
4
Confluence creates confidence. ▼
Never set a stop using a single reference. Require 2+ of: SMA, trend line, support zone, Fibonacci, AVP. A stop level backed by multiple confluences has a much higher probability of holding.
5
Support zones are ranges, not numbers. ▼
Always use the lower bound of the zone as your reference. A support "level" at $48 is really a zone between $47.50 and $48.50 — your stop must clear the bottom of that zone to survive normal volatility.

Validation: A support zone should be confirmed by as many price rejections (touches) as possible. 3+ rejections at minimum is preferable — the more touches, the stronger the zone.
6
Let the stop be the exit. ▼
Never sell a rising stock. The stop loss is the only sell signal (except earnings protection and time stops). This is the Darvas principle: if the stock is going up, stay in. Let the trailing stop do the work of deciding when the trend is over.
7
Document every adjustment. ▼
Log every phase transition in TradingView notes / Trading Playbook / Excel with date, price, and reason. This builds your personal database of decisions and outcomes, making you a better trader over time.

The 4-Phase Execution Protocol

01 Initial Entry ▼
TriggerValid buy signal — breakout from base, Darvas box break, trend line bounce, or SMA150/50 reclaim.
Stop LossStrongest confluence support minus <1× ATR(14). Target stop distance ~4% from entry. Standard maximum 6%.
Volatile ExceptionFor high-volatility stocks where ATR demands a wider stop (up to 8-10%), reduce position size proportionally so dollar risk per portion stays at $2K. The wider the stop, the smaller the position.
Position SizeAllocate by portions (1 portion = ~1% of portfolio, ~$40K). Each portion carries $2K max risk regardless of stop distance. Conviction determines portions:
  • 1 portion (~$40K at 6% stop) → $2K risk
  • 2 portions (~$80K at 6% stop) → $4K risk
  • 3 portions (~$120K at 6% stop) → $6K risk
  • If stop is 10% due to volatility, 1 portion shrinks to ~$20K to keep risk at $2K
SharesAllowed risk ÷ (Entry Price − Stop Loss) = number of shares.
ReferenceMinimum 2 confluences required from: support/resistance zone, SMA (200/150/50/20), validated trend line (3+ touches), Fibonacci 38.2%/50%/61.8%, Anchored Volume Profile.
Purpose: Define risk from the start. The dollar risk is fixed per portion ($2K) — the stop distance determines position size, not the other way around.
02 Breakeven Shield ▼
TriggerPrice has risen ≥ 1.5× ATR(14) above entry AND one of the following conditions is met:
  • Standard move: Price pulls back, forms a higher low, and prints at least 1 confirmation candle (green close above prior candle's high). Move stop to breakeven after this confirmation.
  • Runaway move: Price reaches ≥ 3× ATR(14) above entry without pulling back. Move stop to breakeven immediately. When the stock eventually pulls back and forms a confirmed higher low, raise the stop to that higher low minus <1× ATR(14).
Stop LossEntry Price + 0.5% buffer. When a confirmed higher low forms above breakeven, raise to that higher low minus <1× ATR(14).
Key ConditionNever move to breakeven on the first push up alone. You need either a pullback + higher low + 1 reversal confirmation candle, OR a runaway extension ≥ 3× ATR.
ReferenceBreakout level, higher low structure, swing low minus ATR buffer.
Purpose: Eliminate the risk of a loss. After this phase, the trade is "free."
03 Trend Capture ▼
TriggerPrice has reclaimed and closed above SMA50 for at least 3 consecutive trading days.
Stop LossTrail below SMA50 minus 0.5× ATR(14). The stop only moves up, never down.
Key ConditionBefore trailing the SMA50, the stock must have pulled back to it and shown a reversal — at least 1 confirmation candle (green close above prior candle's high) after touching or approaching the SMA50. If the stock is riding well above SMA50 without any pullback, keep the Phase 02 stop until the first meaningful pullback to SMA50.
ConfluenceBefore finalizing the stop level, verify the SMA50 reference aligns with at least one additional confluence: support zone, trend line, Fibonacci level, or AVP.
ScalingHold full position. This is the core trend phase — let it work.
ReferenceSMA50, swing lows, trend line, confluence support.
Purpose: Ride the core trend using SMA50 as the primary trailing reference. This phase applies while price is within 3× ATR(14) of the SMA50. When price extends beyond 3× ATR above SMA50, the stock is ultra-extended and transitions to Phase 04.
04 Extended Move Defense ▼
TriggerPrice is >3× ATR(14) above SMA50. The stock is ultra-extended.
ActionImmediately split the position 50/50, each half managed with its own trailing strategy:

Aggressive Half (50%) — Tight Momentum Trail

  • If price is respecting SMA20 → trail below SMA20 − 0.5× ATR(14)
  • If price is extremely extended and respecting EMA9 → trail below EMA9 − 0.5× ATR(14)
  • If price is parabolic (extended even beyond EMA9) → trail below the most recent swing low minus 0.5× ATR(14). Update as each new higher swing low forms.
  • Before trailing an SMA/EMA, the stock must have pulled back to it and shown a reversal — at least 1 confirmation candle (green close above prior candle's high). If no pullback yet, park stop at the most recent swing low minus 0.5× ATR(14) and wait.
  • When this stop is hit → sell this 50%. The anchor half remains unchanged.

Anchor Half (50%) — Chandelier Stop (3× ATR from Peak)

  • Place stop at 3× ATR(14) below the most recent highest high (peak price).
  • This stop ratchets up only: as the stock makes new highs, recalculate from the new peak. The stop never moves down.
  • If the stock drops from its peak without making new highs, the stop stays at the level calculated from the last peak.
  • This half rides through consolidations and pullbacks that would trigger the aggressive trail.
  • Transition back to Phase 03: When SMA50 catches up (price returns to within normal range of SMA50, i.e., <3× ATR above it), this half transitions back to Phase 03 rules — trail below SMA50 − 0.5× ATR(14).
ConfluenceVerify trailing references align with at least one additional confluence before finalizing stop levels.
ReferenceSMA20, EMA9, swing lows, chandelier exit from highest high, ATR distance from SMA50.
Purpose: Extended moves are where the big money is made. The aggressive half captures the momentum break early with tight trailing (SMA20 → EMA9 → swing low). The anchor half, inspired by Darvas's patience and LeBeau's chandelier exit, gives the position room to breathe through consolidations while the SMA50 catches up — ensuring you stay in for the full trend.

Annexes

A Time Stop (Dead Money Rule) ▼
Trigger15 trading days (~3 calendar weeks) after entry with no meaningful price progress. "No progress" = price has not moved > 1× ATR(14) above entry price.
ActionSell full position at market. Move ticker to watchlist for re-entry if it sets up again.
Purpose: Capital has opportunity cost. Money sitting in a stock that isn't moving could be deployed into a better setup. If the stock hasn't responded within 3 weeks of your entry signal, the thesis is not playing out on your timeframe.
B Earnings Protection ▼
TriggerEarnings date approaching. Act 1 trading day before the report.
ActionDepends on unrealized gain:
  • Position at a loss or breakeven → Sell 100%. No exceptions.
  • Small gain (under 10%) → Sell 100%. Cushion too thin to absorb a gap down.
  • Moderate gain (10-20%) → Sell 50%. Tighten stop on remaining 50% to breakeven.
  • Large gain (20%+, core position) → Hold. Tighten stop to below the pre-earnings swing low or nearest key SMA, whichever is closer.
Timing1 trading day before the report. Pay attention to whether earnings are BMO (before market open) or AMC (after market close) and adjust accordingly.
Post-Earnings Re-EntryIf you sold and the stock gaps up, do NOT chase. Wait for a new setup — pullback, flag, or new base. Treat re-entry as a fresh Phase 01 trade.
Post-Earnings ExitIf you held through and the stock gaps down but holds above your stop, reassess. If the stock reclaims pre-earnings levels within 3 trading days, resume previous phase trailing. If it doesn't, exit on the third day.
Purpose: Earnings are binary events — stops cannot protect against overnight gaps. The profit cushion determines the action, not a prediction about the results.
C Market Regime Overlay ▼
ReferenceS&P 500 vs. its SMA50, SMA150, and SMA200.
Regime Rules
  • Healthy (S&P above SMA150, SMA150 rising) → Normal operations. Full exposure allowed. All phases apply. New entries permitted.
  • Caution (S&P closes below SMA50) → No new entries. Review all open positions. Tighten Phase 04 stops by one level. Phases 01-03 unchanged.
  • Deteriorating (S&P closes below SMA150) → No new entries. Reduce total exposure to max 50%. Sell weakest positions first (smallest cushion, earliest phase). Raise all stops one phase tighter.
  • Bear (S&P closes below SMA200) → Raise 75%+ to cash. Only hold positions in Phase 03 or 04 with large cushions (20%+). Everything else closed.
RecoveryWhen S&P reclaims SMA150 with 3 consecutive daily closes above it, begin rebuilding positions gradually. Start with 1-portion positions only until the trend re-establishes.
Purpose: Individual stock stops don't operate in a vacuum. This overlay adjusts exposure and behavior across all open positions based on market health.
